# Busch School Course Planning Bot — Project Brief

Undergraduate students at the Tim & Steph Busch School of Business at The Catholic University of America frequently struggle to navigate the complexity of their degree requirements, specialization options, minor pairings, and administrative processes — often resulting in delayed graduation, missed requirements, and unnecessary stress. Academic advisors face high advising loads and repetitive questions that consume time better spent on nuanced, high-stakes student conversations. The proposed solution is the Busch School Course Planning Bot, a web-based AI advisor built specifically for Busch undergraduate students pursuing B.S.B.A. or B.S. in Accounting degrees. Students can access the tool via any browser, upload their Academic Planning Worksheet, and receive instant, personalized guidance on degree progress, course sequencing, specialization requirements, minor options, and how to submit administrative forms — all without waiting for an appointment. AI plays the central role in this solution, serving as a knowledgeable first-line advisor trained on the complete official Busch School curriculum, prerequisite chains, catalog-year distinctions, and university policies, enabling it to answer the majority of routine advising questions accurately and immediately while freeing human advisors to focus on complex cases that require professional judgment.
